Overview

This is an overview of my first co-op experience as a Monitoring & Analytics student at Canadian Solar Solutions Inc. from September 2018 to December 2018.

About the company

Canadian Solar Solutions Inc. is a global company based in Guelph, Ontario, Canada which engages in manufacturing solar photovoltaic (PV) modules and provides solar energy solutions worldwide. It offers solar cells, solar modules, solar power systems, off-grid home application system and other specialized solar products.

The office located in Guelph has a manufacturing facility where solar cells, solar PV modules, etc. are manufactured. I had the opportunity to have a tour around the facility and witness the production of solar panels.

The department where I was assigned during my co-op (Operations and Maintenance, O&M) consists of people from different backgrounds: control room operators, system performance analysts, developers, engineers, etc. working closely together to provide monitoring, diagnostics and troubleshooting of solar plants by using one of their software, namely CSEye.

To learn more about Canadian Solar Operations and Maintenance., click here: https://www.canadiansolar.com/en/energy-solutions/om-services.html

Job Description

My project at Canadian Solar Inc. was to develop an Operator Log page for auditing and NERC compliance purposes.

Control room operators monitor and troubleshoot events that are happening at different solar farm sites on a 24/7/365 basis.

Hence, the Operator Log is mostly used by operators to record and keep track of events and interactions happening at different solar farm sites. And since they work on shifts, one operator can easily reviewed what happened in the previous shift through the Operator Log. Even though operators are the only ones able to enter new log, other member in the O&M department can use the Operator Log for auditing.

The Operator Log consists of two parts:

  1. New Log:
    • Only operators can create new event for different sites
  2. Log History:
    • Users(Regular users and Operators) can view all the logs from the previous date to the current date
    • All users can filter the logs based on operator’s name, site, date and log description. They are also able to sort each column by ascending or descending order.
    • Operators have additional features such as editing a log, create a log thread which groups all the related logs together.
